Interesting Facts about Camel

The feet of the camel are flat to enable them to walk on the sand in the desert without sinking. The camel has a hump. In some regions, the camels might even have two humps. The hump is the place where the camel stores its food and water for days together as there is a dearth of the same in the desert regions.

Interesting Facts about chameleon

A very talented reptile type of animal species is the Chameleon. The scientific name of Chameleon is Chamaeleonidae sp. This lizard species has inhabited warmer places all around the world. Chameleons are characterized by a small or big body with four feet and five toes on each. The Chameleon head consists of two big eyes, which is rotatable. The tail of Chameleons can also be curled, and the skin of chameleons is dry and scaled. Most Chameleon species can change their skin color for camouflage.

Interesting Facts about panda

The panda's eyes, ears, legs, and shoulders are all black, and the rest of its body is white. Although fairly big, the giant panda isn't really all that giant. It can grow up to about three feet tall and six feet long when standing on all four legs. The female pandas are generally smaller than the males.

Interesting Facts about pigeon

Pigeons are intelligent birds. They can identify them on the mirror. ... Pigeons can easily detect distant storms, earthquakes and volcanic eruptions since they have a very sharp sense of hearing. Pigeons can carry messages to distant places and they were used as messengers during many wars.
